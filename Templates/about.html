About This Project

Requirements

This project required that we produce at least three interactive visualizations, using a dataset with at least 100 records, and utilzing the following technologies:
  • Python Flask-powered RESTful API
  • HTML/CSS
  • Javascript
  • One relational or non-relational database

Our Approach

We started with a minimal dataset - just 5 columns and 2000 rows - easilly exported from the CDC website in .csv format.

After some initial exploration, analysis, and cleanup of the raw data using Jupyter Notebook, we decided to pull our data into a SQLite database, due to the overall portability and easy accessibility of SQLite via Flask app.

In Flask, we created multiple routes to serve up variations of our data in the JSON format necessary to feed our visualizations. Ultimately, we used a combination of JSON and .csv data to populate our charts.

The charts use D3, Plotly, and a technology new to all of us: Observable.

To style our HTML, we liked the idea of using a free Bootstrap theme; what you see on our site is the Lux theme, customized to fit our needs.

Overall our goal was to keep things simple and to concentrate the bulk of our efforts on the visualizations.
